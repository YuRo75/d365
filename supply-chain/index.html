1 What is Supply Chain Management?

Dynamics 365 Supply Chain Management helps organizations manufacture and move goods — connecting inventory, warehousing, production, and planning so you can meet demand efficiently and see the whole chain from procurement to delivery.

Enterprise ERPLike Finance, SCM runs on the Finance & Operations platform and is built for large, complex operations.

2 The core areas

Inventory

Track what you have, where, and its value.

Warehouse

Receive, put away, pick, and ship with mobile-driven processes.

Production

Plan and execute manufacturing orders.

Planning

Master planning balances supply and demand.

Procurement

Source and purchase materials from vendors.

Sales & delivery

Fulfil customer orders from stock or production.

3 Warehouse management

Warehouse management controls the physical flow of goods through your facilities — from receiving to shipping — using configurable work and mobile devices on the floor.

StepWhat happens
ReceiveGoods arrive and are registered against a purchase order.
Put awayItems are directed to storage locations.
PickItems are gathered to fulfil an order.
Pack & shipOrders are packed and dispatched to customers.

4 Planning & production

Master planning looks at demand (orders, forecasts) and supply (stock, purchases, production) and proposes what to make or buy, and when. Production orders then execute the manufacturing.

Balance supply & demandGood planning avoids both stockouts (lost sales) and excess inventory (tied-up cash). It's the engine that keeps the chain efficient.
