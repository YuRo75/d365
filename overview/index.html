1 What is Dynamics 365?

Microsoft Dynamics 365 is a portfolio of intelligent business applications that span customer relationship management (CRM) and enterprise resource planning (ERP). Each app targets a business function — sales, service, finance, supply chain — but they share one data foundation and one set of platform services, so they work as a connected whole.

CRM apps

Engage customers: Sales, Customer Service, Field Service, Customer Insights.

ERP apps

Run operations: Finance, Supply Chain Management, Business Central.

Shared foundation

All built on Dataverse + Power Platform, with Copilot across every app.
One platform, many appsUnlike buying disconnected systems, Dynamics 365 apps share data, security, and automation. A Sales opportunity, a Service case, and a Finance invoice can reference the same customer record.

2 CRM vs ERP — the two halves

CRM (Customer Engagement)ERP (Finance & Operations)

Which ERP?Finance + Supply Chain Management target large enterprises; Business Central is the all-in-one ERP optimized for small and medium businesses.

3 The shared foundation

Most Dynamics 365 CRM apps are model-driven apps built on Microsoft Dataverse and the Power Platform. That foundation is what makes them connected and extensible.

Dataverse

The secure, cloud data store where business records (tables) live.

Power Platform

Power Apps, Power Automate, Power BI and Copilot Studio extend and automate.

Microsoft 365

Deep ties to Outlook, Teams, Excel, and SharePoint.

5 Licensing & access

  • Apps are licensed per user, per app (you buy only what each role needs).
  • A Power Platform admin center and the Microsoft 365 admin center manage environments and users.
  • Most CRM apps run in a Dataverse environment; Finance & Operations apps use their own infrastructure.
  • Security is role-based, scoped down to table, row, and field level.
